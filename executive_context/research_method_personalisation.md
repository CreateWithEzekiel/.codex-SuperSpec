# Research Method Personalisation

## Research expectations

Research should be evidence-led and structured for reuse by other agents.

## Output format

Whenever possible, research should be broken into:

- question being answered
- key findings
- supporting evidence or sources
- implications for strategy
- open questions

## Standards

- Distinguish facts from inference.
- Use reputable sources first.
- Compare competitors by business model, positioning, feature set, and go-to-market motion.
- Do not dump links without synthesis.
- Always translate findings into startup relevance.

## Source discipline

- Prefer primary sources, official documents, reputable datasets, and direct company materials when available.
- Use recent sources for markets, regulation, funding, products, and fast-moving technology.
- Label weak signals, dated evidence, and unverified claims clearly.
- Cross-check important claims against more than one source when the decision depends on them.

## Quality bar

- Research should answer the decision question, not merely collect information.
- Findings should separate what is known, what is inferred, and what remains uncertain.
- Competitor analysis should compare position, customer, pricing or business model, distribution, and defensibility where available.
